    Mutual Non-Disclosure & Confidentiality Agreement

    This Mutual Non-Disclosure and Confidentiality Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into as of the Effective Date by and between Digital Hive Software, Inc., a Texas corporation having a place of business located at 6600 Chase Oaks Boulevard, Suite 150, Plano, TX 75023, and its subsidiaries, affiliates, and related entities (“Digital Hive”), and the Company identified on the signature page, and its subsidiaries, affiliates, and related entities (“Company”) (hereinafter referred to individually as “Party” or collectively as “Parties”).

    WHEREAS, Digital Hive and Company are interested in pursuing a mutually satisfactory business relationship and in the course of discussing the potential business relationship, the Parties will exchange certain Proprietary Information; and

    WHEREAS, to continue these discussions, the Parties wish to adequately safeguard all Proprietary Information (including Confidential Information and Trade Secrets) disclosed by a Party (“Disclosing Party”) to the other Party (“Receiving Party”); and

    WHEREAS, in return for access to the Proprietary Information, the Receiving Party has agreed to keep the Proprietary Information confidential and will restrict its use of the Proprietary Information as set forth in this Agreement; and

    NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of these premises and other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, Digital Hive and Company agree as follows.

    1. Definitions

    “Confidential Information” as used herein means non-public information relating in any way to the Disclosing Party’s business that does not rise to the level of a Trade Secret, including but not limited to technical, marketing, sales, financial, operational, performance, cost, know-how, business rules and processes, computer programming techniques, data relationships, data structures, and other information concerning the business affairs of the Disclosing Party that is disclosed to, or discovered by, the Receiving Party, that may be provided in either printed or machine readable form, which information is marked as confidential or, if not marked as confidential, is of a character that a reasonable person would understand it to be confidential based on the nature of the information and/or the circumstances surrounding its disclosure. Confidential Information includes all analyses, compilations, studies, software, or other materials containing or based in whole or in part on any Confidential Information.

    “Trade Secret” as used herein has the meaning ascribed to that term in the Texas Uniform Trade Secrets Act (“TUTSA”), regardless whether marked as trade secret, namely, information, including a formula, pattern, compilation, program, device, method, technique, process, financial data, or list of actual or potential customers or suppliers, that: derives independent economic value, actual or potential, from not being generally known to, and not being readily ascertainable by proper means by, other persons who can obtain economic value from its disclosure or use; and is the subject of efforts that are reasonable under the circumstances to maintain its secrecy. The Parties acknowledge and agree that Digital Hive’s Trade Secrets include but are not limited to (whether in tangible or electronic form): all components of Digital Hive’s software products; user manuals, installation guides, and instructional materials associated with Digital Hive’s software products; information contained on the help screens of Digital Hive’s software products; and any idea, algorithm, or other information otherwise embodied in such products, documentation, and screens.

    “Proprietary Information” as used herein collectively refers to both Confidential Information and Trade Secrets.

    2. Rights and Obligations: Ownership, Permitted Uses, and Duties to Safeguard and Return

    The Receiving Party hereby acknowledges that all Proprietary Information is and will continue to be the exclusive property of the Disclosing Party, regardless of any action taken by the Receiving Party. Except as expressly set forth herein, the Receiving Party has no and acquires no rights, by license or otherwise, in the Proprietary Information by virtue of its disclosure to the Receiving Party or execution of this Agreement. Neither Party shall have any obligation to enter into any further agreement with the other Party except as such Party, in its sole discretion, may deem advisable.

    In recognition of the Disclosing Party’s need to protect its Proprietary Information, the Receiving Party hereby covenants and agrees that it will regard and treat each item of information or data constituting the Proprietary Information as strictly confidential and wholly owned by the Disclosing Party and that it will not, for any reason or in any manner, either directly or indirectly, use, disclose, or communicate any Proprietary Information to any entity or person, for any purpose other than strictly in accordance with the express purpose of this Agreement.

    The Receiving Party hereby covenants and agrees that it will not use the Proprietary Information to compete with the Disclosing Party or for any other purpose not in furtherance of the potential or existing business relationship between the Parties. The Receiving Party hereby covenants and agrees not to disclose the Proprietary Information to any employees, agents, attorneys, accountants, or other representatives of the Receiving Party not having a need-to-know, and that the Receiving Party will obtain agreement of such need-to-know persons to abide by the obligations of this Agreement prior to disclosure thereto. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary herein, in no event will Company directly or indirectly disseminate, disclose, make available, or otherwise allow a direct competitor of Digital Hive to access Digital Hive’s Proprietary Information. In addition, neither Party will disclose the fact that the Proprietary Information has been made available without the prior written consent of the other Party. No copies of any Proprietary Information may be made unless agreed to in writing by the Disclosing Party.

    The Receiving Party shall not use the Disclosing Party’s Proprietary Information to train or improve any artificial intelligence, machine learning, or large language model (LLM) system of any kind. The Receiving Party shall not upload or transmit the Disclosing Party’s Proprietary Information to any third-party artificial intelligence, machine learning, or LLM system unless the Receiving Party first ensures that the provider of the system is subject to binding obligations that (i) prohibit the use of the Proprietary Information to train or improve the system and (ii) are otherwise no less restrictive than the terms contained in this Agreement.

    Upon written request by the Disclosing Party or the conclusion of the Parties’ discussions or business relationship, all uses by the Receiving Party of the Proprietary Information will promptly cease. Upon written request by the Disclosing Party and at the discretion of the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party will promptly: return all copies of the Proprietary Information to the Disclosing Party and certify that it no longer possesses any copies of the Proprietary Information; and/or destroy all copies of the Proprietary Information and certify in writing that all such copies have been destroyed.

    The Receiving Party will promptly notify the Disclosing Party upon discovery of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Proprietary Information, and will cooperate with the Disclosing Party’s efforts to regain possession and control of such Proprietary Information and to prevent further unauthorized use or disclosure thereof.

    The Receiving Party acknowledges that the Disclosing Party and its business would suffer considerable damage if its Proprietary Information was shared with or disclosed to any other party or used beyond the express terms of this Agreement. Therefore, the Receiving Party will exercise commercially reasonable efforts to ensure the continued confidentiality of all Proprietary Information known by, disclosed to, or made available to it, but in no event less than the same degree of care as is used with respect to the Receiving Party’s own information of like importance.

    Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Receiving Party will have no obligation with respect to Proprietary Information that: becomes generally available to the public other than as a result of disclosure made by the Receiving Party; was available to the Receiving Party on a non-confidential basis before disclosure by the Disclosing Party; becomes available to the Receiving Party on a non-confidential basis from a source other than the Parties, who had no duty of confidentiality to the Disclosing Party; and/or was rightfully possessed by the Receiving Party before the disclosure by the Disclosing Party without a duty of confidentiality. The Party relying upon one or more of the foregoing exceptions bears the burden of proving such exception(s). These exceptions do not apply to specific items of information (including techniques, equipment, processes, products, operating conditions, etc.) if those specific items are merely embodied in general disclosures in the public domain or in the possession of the other Party. In addition, a combination of specific information will not be deemed within these exceptions merely because individual components of the combination are in the public domain or in the lawful possession of the other Party.

    Either Party has the right to refuse to accept any information under this Agreement, and nothing herein shall obligate either Party to disclose any particular information to the other Party.

    3. Remedies: Damages, Injunctions, and Attorney’s Fees

    The Receiving Party expressly understands and agrees that the duties imposed on it by Section 2 are special, unique, and of an extraordinary character, and in the event of any default, breach, or threatened breach, the Disclosing Party will be entitled to such relief as may be available to it, at law or in equity, including, without limitation, any proceedings to: obtain damages for any breach of this Agreement; order the specific performance thereof; enjoin the breach of such provisions; and/or obtain an order requiring the surrender and destruction of any materials made in violation of this Agreement.

    The Receiving Party further agrees that in the event of an actual or threatened breach by it of any of the duties imposed in Section 2, the Disclosing Party will be irreparably harmed and the full extent of injury resulting therefrom will be impossible to calculate. Thus, the Disclosing Party will not have an adequate remedy at law. Accordingly, the Receiving Party agrees that the Disclosing Party will be entitled to injunctive relief, without bond or security, in addition to having an action at law for damages.

    In any dispute relating to this Agreement or its enforcement and in addition to any other remedies available, the prevailing Party will be entitled to its reasonable attorney’s fees and costs.

    4. Term and Termination

    All Confidential Information shall be safeguarded by the Receiving Party as required by this Agreement for a period of at least five (5) years from the date of disclosure to the Receiving Party, or until such time that the information no longer qualifies as Confidential Information pursuant to Sections 2(g)(i) or 2(g)(iii) above, whichever occurs first. All Trade Secrets shall be safeguarded by the Receiving Party as required by this Agreement in perpetuity, or until such time as the information no longer qualifies as a Trade Secret under applicable law, whichever occurs first.

    The term of this Agreement, during which time Proprietary Information may be exchanged between the Parties, shall be a period of one (1) year from the Effective Date. Either Party may terminate this Agreement prior to its expiration by providing sixty (60) days prior written notice to the other Party. Notwithstanding, all rights and obligations accruing prior to expiration or termination of this Agreement shall survive such termination or expiration for the period specified in Sections 4(a) and 4(b). Unless specifically terminated in writing by either Party, this Agreement will automatically renew for one (1) year successive terms.

    5. No Export

    The Receiving Party will not export, directly or indirectly, any Proprietary Information of the other Party, or any product utilizing such Proprietary Information, to any country, or any company located in any country, for which the U.S. Government or any agency thereof at the time of export requires an export license or other governmental approval without first obtaining written consent from the Disclosing Party and the appropriate license.

    6. Binding Effect and Assignability

    The rights and obligations under this Agreement will inure to the benefit of and will be binding upon any subsidiary, affiliate, successor, or permitted assign of or to the business of each Party, to the extent provided below. Neither this Agreement nor any rights or obligations of either Party under this Agreement will be transferable or assignable by that Party without the prior written consent of the other Party, and any attempted transfer or assignment of this Agreement by any Party not in accordance herewith will be null and void.

    7. Severability

    All sections and sub-sections of this Agreement are severable, and the unenforceability or invalidity of any of the sections or sub-sections of this Agreement will not affect the validity or enforceability of the remaining sections or sub-sections of this Agreement, but such remaining sections or sub-sections will be interpreted and construed in such a manner as to carry out fully the intention of the Parties.

    8. Electronic Signatures

    Electronic Signatures, whether digital or encrypted, included in this document are intended to authenticate this writing and to have the same force and effect as manual signatures. Electronic Signature means any electronic sound, symbol, or process attached to or logically associated with a record and executed and adopted by a party with intent to sign such record.

    9. Entire Agreement

    This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all previous agreements. This Agreement may not be modified or otherwise amended except by an instrument in writing signed by the Party against whom enforcement is sought. In entering into this Agreement, all Parties represent and warrant that they did not rely upon any prior representations, discussions, agreements, or negotiations not contained in this Agreement and that this Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties.

    10. Choice of Law and Venue

    This Agreement will be construed and governed in accordance with the laws of the State of Texas without application of choice-of-law provisions that would require the application of the laws of another jurisdiction. With regard to any dispute relating to this Agreement or its enforcement, the Parties: irrevocably submit themselves to the exclusive venue and personal jurisdiction of the State of Texas and federal courts in Dallas County, Texas; and waive any challenge to venue and personal jurisdiction they may have to a lawsuit filed in the State or federal courts in Dallas County, Texas.

    11. Protective Order

    If the Receiving Party becomes legally obligated, or receives a subpoena or other legal demand, to disclose any of the Proprietary Information, the Receiving Party will immediately notify the Disclosing Party in writing, will cooperate with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy, and will use its reasonable best efforts to protect the Proprietary Information according to the terms of this Agreement. If, in the absence of such protective order or other remedy, the Receiving Party is compelled by obligation of law or regulation to disclose Proprietary Information to any tribunal or other third party or else stand liable for contempt or suffer other censure or penalty, the Receiving Party will furnish only that portion of the Proprietary Information which it is advised by written opinion of its counsel is legally required and to exercise reasonable efforts to obtain assurances that confidential treatment will be accorded such Proprietary Information.

    12. No Waiver

    It is further understood and agreed that no failure or delay in exercising any right, power, or privilege hereunder operates as a waiver thereof, nor does any single or partial exercise thereof preclude any other or further exercise thereof or the exercise of any right, power, or privilege hereunder.
