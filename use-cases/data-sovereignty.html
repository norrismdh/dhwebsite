What actually happens

What a compliance request actually requires.

Legal needs to confirm only authorized users can access personal data. Simple question. The answer requires logging into five BI admin consoles, cross-referencing export logs, and assembling a report that's already out of date before it's submitted.

Each tool has its own access model. Its own audit log. None of them talk to each other.

Why this doesn't get fixed

Three constraints that are real, not excuses.

Most catalog tools ingest metadata by moving or copying it — creating the compliance risk they're supposed to prevent.

  • Most catalog tools ingest metadata by moving or copying it — creating the compliance risk they're supposed to prevent.
  • Source-platform security is often not preserved through an abstraction layer.
  • Governance and data sovereignty get treated as competing requirements. They're not.
  • Audit readiness is reactive — assembled under pressure after an incident, not maintained continuously.
What changes

How Digital Hive solves it.

Four shifts that govern without disrupting.

Zero data movement.

Digital Hive indexes metadata only. Reports and data stay in their native platforms — nothing copied or moved.

Source security inherited.

Permissions defined in each BI platform are respected automatically. Nothing overwritten.

Audit-ready governance.

Certification workflows, usage logs, and lineage tracking across all platforms in one place.

On-premises deployment available.

For environments where cloud SaaS isn't permitted, self-hosted deployment is supported.
