What actually happens

The packaging problem that creates the trust problem.

Analysts pull numbers from multiple systems, assemble decks, and distribute on a schedule. By the time the executive reads it, it's stale. When two leaders compare notes and the numbers differ - different tools, different time windows - strategy conversations become data reconciliation conversations.

The CFO has one set of numbers. The COO has another. The CEO is asking which one to trust. The answer - that they're both right but measuring slightly different things - isn't one anyone wants to give in a board meeting.

Why this doesn't get fixed

Why executive reporting keeps breaking at the worst moments.

Push delivery fails at edge cases. No single analyst has a complete picture across fragmented BI tools.

  • Push delivery fails at edge cases: Sunday evenings, board meetings, absent report authors.
  • No single analyst has a complete picture across fragmented BI tools.
  • Certification is informal. Executives rely on memory and context clues to identify the "official" version.
  • Trust erodes slowly. One "which number is right?" moment creates skepticism that outlasts the incident.
