What actually happens

The parallel-platform problem

New platform selected. Migration begins. Immediately two worlds exist: legacy reports on the old system, new content on the modern one. Users bounce between both, unsure which version is current

Adoption stalls - not because the new platform is bad, but because the old one still has what people need

Why this doesn't get fixed

Why modernization projects run 18 months longer than planned

  • Migrations are scoped as technology replacements. Users don't follow content - they follow familiarity
  • Business continuity keeps the legacy platform live long after the planned cutover
  • During the parallel window, users learn two systems. IT supports two systems
  • Adoption is measured by who's been migrated - not by who is using the new platform

How Digital Hive solves it

Four shifts that separate the user experience from the migration timeline
